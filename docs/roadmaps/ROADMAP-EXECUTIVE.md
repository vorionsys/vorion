# Vorion for Executives

> **Read time**: 5 minutes | **Audience**: CEOs, CTOs, Board Members

## The Problem on Your Balance Sheet

Every AI agent your company deploys today operates without governance infrastructure. No identity. No behavioral constraints. No audit trail. When regulators ask "which AI made that decision and under what authority?" — your team has no answer.

This is not a future problem. The EU AI Act is enforceable. The NIST AI RMF is published. Your competitors are scrambling. The question isn't whether AI governance is coming — it's whether you'll build it yourself, buy it from someone who doesn't understand your stack, or adopt the open standard that the ecosystem is converging on.

## What Vorion Is

Think of it as **Okta for AI agents**.

Okta gives every human an identity, a set of permissions, and an audit log. Vorion does the same for AI agents:

| Human (Okta) | AI Agent (Vorion) |
|-------------|-------------------|
| SSO identity | CAR ID (Categorical Agentic Registry) |
| RBAC permissions | BASIS constraints (behavioral boundaries) |
| Access logs | PROOF chain (immutable, dual-hash audit trail) |
| Compliance reports | Automated compliance mapping (EU AI Act, NIST AI RMF) |

## How It Works (No Jargon)

1. **Register** — Every agent gets a unique identity (like an employee badge)
2. **Constrain** — Set rules for what the agent can and cannot do (like job responsibilities)
3. **Verify** — Every action is checked against the rules in real-time (like badge access at every door)
4. **Prove** — Every decision is recorded in a tamper-proof chain (like security camera footage)

## The Trust Model

Agents earn autonomy through demonstrated reliability — an 8-tier model (T0-T7) where new agents start restricted and earn broader operational latitude through consistent, governed behavior. This is not "AI alignment" — it's behavioral reliability measurement, the same principle as credit scores or employee performance reviews.

| Tier | Analogy | What It Means |
|------|---------|---------------|
| T0-T1 | New intern | Every action supervised |
| T2-T3 | Junior employee | Standard tasks approved automatically |
| T4-T5 | Senior manager | Can make decisions within defined boundaries |
| T6-T7 | Trusted executive | Broad autonomy, monitored for drift |
