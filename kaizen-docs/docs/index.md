## What is Agentic AI?

Agentic AI refers to AI systems that can **autonomously perceive, reason, plan, and act** to achieve goals with minimal human intervention. Unlike traditional AI that responds to single prompts, agentic systems:

- **Persist** across interactions, maintaining state and memory
- **Plan** multi-step actions to achieve complex objectives
- **Use tools** to interact with external systems and APIs
- **Adapt** based on feedback and environmental changes
- **Collaborate** with other agents and humans

## Knowledge Domains

### [Agent Taxonomy](/taxonomy)
Classification of autonomous agents from simple reflex to cognitive architectures. Understand the spectrum from reactive scripts to BDI agents.

### [Cognitive Architecture](/architecture)
The internal anatomy: memory systems, reasoning engines, planning algorithms, and tool integration patterns.

### [Orchestration](/orchestration)
How multiple agents collaborate: hierarchical control, swarm intelligence, event-driven coordination, and consensus protocols.

### [Protocols & Standards](/protocols)
The interoperability layer: MCP (Model Context Protocol), A2A (Agent-to-Agent), decentralized identity, and the ACI governance specification.

### [Domain Applications](/domains)
Specialized implementations in software engineering, scientific research, financial trading, and enterprise automation.

### [Agent Evolution](/evolution)
How agents are initialized, optimized, and improved over time through evolutionary algorithms and memetic learning.

### [Safety & Governance](/safety)
Trust scoring, capability gating, audit trails, and human oversight mechanisms for responsible agent deployment.
