## Technique Sessions

### Technique 1: First Principles Thinking

**Goal:** Strip away assumptions to identify fundamental requirements for bots running business functions

**Core Insights Generated:**

**Fundamental Truth #1: Execute with Ownership and Integrity**
- Businesses fundamentally require accountability and trustworthiness
- Bots must "own" their domain with integrity (no passing the buck)
- This is deeper than just "completing tasks" - it's about responsibility

**Fundamental Truth #2: Follow Processes and SOPs, Execute Skills**
- Business operations are systematic (processes/SOPs exist for a reason)
- Bots need both: adherence to process AND skill execution
- Not just rules-followers - must have competencies to execute within those processes

**Fundamental Truth #3: Evaluate Risk, Minimize It, Maximize Value**
- Every business decision involves risk assessment
- Bots must balance risk mitigation with value creation
- This is the decision-making core: risk vs. reward optimization

**Deep Dive Questions:**

**Q: How far does bot ownership extend?**

A: Ownership evolves through confidence levels:
- **Learning Phase**: Bot asks, then suggests, tracks approval rates
- **Autonomous Phase**: When suggestions achieve high enough approval rate, bot owns the decision
- **Evaluation**: Bots own metrics and outcomes (e.g., Customer Success Bot owns NPS/retention)
- **Ownership = Accountability for Results**, not just task completion

**Q: What skills must business-operating bots have?**

A: Core skill framework:
- **Self-Training**: Bots can seek ongoing outside training autonomously
- **Skill Creation**: Some bots can CREATE skills and entire ecosystems
- **Meta-Learning**: Learning how to learn and evolve capabilities
- **Tool Mastery**: Communication, data analysis, system integration
- **Collective Intelligence**: Leverage multi-bot review and guidance

**Q: How does a bot evaluate risk?**

A: Multi-layered risk framework:
- **Three-Bot Review Process**: Before escalating to next level, 3 bots review decisions
- **Hierarchical Escalation**: Complex/high-risk questions filter up through bot management layers
- **HITL Safety Net**: Human-in-the-loop available to help or source further answers
- **Imaginary Council**: Bots can seek guidance from simulated expert council (different perspectives)
- **Risk Scoring**: Financial, reputation, operational, strategic risk quantification
- **Confidence Thresholds**: Risk level determines required confidence for autonomous action

**Revolutionary Concepts Identified:**

1. **Graduated Autonomy System**: Ask → Suggest → Track Approval → Autonomy when confidence threshold met
2. **Bot-Created Ecosystems**: Bots that create other bots, skills, and systems
3. **Three-Bot Review**: Peer review before escalation (distributed decision validation)
4. **Imaginary Council**: Simulated expert perspectives for guidance
5. **Self-Training Bots**: Autonomous learning and skill acquisition

**First Principles Summary - The Minimal Bot Business Operating System:**

A bot can run a business function when it has:
1. **Ownership Framework**: Metrics, accountability, graduated autonomy based on confidence
2. **Skill Engine**: Execute tasks + Self-train + Create new skills/ecosystems
3. **Decision Framework**: Risk evaluation + Multi-bot review + Hierarchical escalation + HITL safety
4. **Process Adherence**: SOPs + Integrity + Continuous improvement
5. **Value Optimization**: Every decision evaluated on risk vs. reward

   - Public display: Score visible to all users

   - Benefit: Faster deployment, identifies true risk areas
